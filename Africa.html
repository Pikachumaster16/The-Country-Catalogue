Africa

Africa is the second‑largest continent, covering about 30.3 million km², covering 20% of Earth’s land area. It is home to over 1.57 billion people, making it the most genetically and culturally diverse region on the planet. It contains 54 countries and a huge range of environments, from the Sahara, the world’s largest hot desert, to the Congo Rainforest, the second‑largest tropical rainforest, and the Nile River, the longest river on Earth. Africa is rich in minerals, biodiversity, and languages (over 2,000), and is the birthplace of humanity, with the oldest known fossils of Homo sapiens found there. Major economic and cultural hubs include Lagos, Cairo, Johannesburg, and Nairobi, while its history spans ancient civilizations like Egypt, Carthage, and Mali to modern, rapidly growing economies.
