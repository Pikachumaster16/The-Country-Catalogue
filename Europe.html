Europe

Europe is a small but densely influential continent of about 10.19 million km², covering 6.8% of the world’s land area. 743 million people live in Europe, having most of its population living in Russia, Germany, the UK etc. Stretching from the Atlantic Ocean to the Ural Mountains, it contains around 44 countries, including major powers like Germany, France, and the UK, and is home to the European Union, one of the world’s largest economic blocs. Shaped by ancient civilizations such as Greece and Rome, Europe has driven global developments in science, art, philosophy, and politics, while its climate—mild for its latitude thanks to the Gulf Stream—supports diverse cultures, languages, and landscapes packed into a relatively compact space.
