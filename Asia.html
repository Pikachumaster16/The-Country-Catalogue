Asia

Asia is the largest and most populous continent, covering about 44.5 million km², covering 30% of Earth’s land area—and home to around 4.7 billion people, more than 60% of the world’s population. It contains 49 countries and extreme geographic diversity, from the Himalayas, the world’s tallest mountains, to the vast Siberian tundra, the Arabian Desert, and the dense rainforests of Southeast Asia. Asia is the birthplace of major world religions—Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, and Christianity—and hosts some of the largest economies, including China, India, Japan, and South Korea. Its cultural, linguistic, and ecological variety is unmatched, spanning thousands of ethnic groups, climates, and ecosystems.
