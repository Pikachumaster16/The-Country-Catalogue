Oceania

Oceania covers about 8.5 million km², being only 5.7% of the world’s land area and includes 14 countries plus thousands of islands spread across the Pacific Ocean, divided into Australia, Melanesia, Micronesia, and Polynesia. It ranges from the massive, resource‑rich continent of Australia to the volcanic peaks of New Zealand and the coral atolls of nations like Kiribati and Tuvalu. Home to around 44 million people, Oceania contains extraordinary biodiversity—from kangaroos and koalas to birds‑of‑paradise—and some of the world’s most unique ecosystems shaped by isolation. Its cultures are equally diverse, with deep Indigenous traditions such as Aboriginal Australian, Māori, and Polynesian heritage, alongside modern urban centers like Sydney, Auckland, and Melbourne.
