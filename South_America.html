South America

South America spans about 17.8 million km², covering 12% of the world’s land area. South America is home to over 430 million people across 12 countries, dominated geographically by the Andes Mountains, the world’s longest continental mountain range, and the Amazon Basin, which contains the planet’s largest rainforest and river system. The continent holds some of Earth’s richest biodiversity, from jaguars to pink river dolphins, and vast natural resources like oil, copper, and freshwater. Major cultural and economic centers include São Paulo, Buenos Aires, Lima, and Bogotá, while Indigenous civilizations such as the Inca Empire shaped its history long before European colonization. South America’s climate ranges from the Atacama Desert, one of the driest places on Earth, to the icy Patagonia region in the south.
