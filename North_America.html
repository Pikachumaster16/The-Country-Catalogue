North America

North America spans 24.7 million km², covering 16.3% of the world’s population. It has 23 countries, from the Arctic islands of Canada and Greenland to the tropical nations of Central America and the Caribbean. Home to around 600 million people, it includes the world’s largest economy (the United States) and the second‑largest landmass (Canada). The continent features some of the planet’s most dramatic natural systems—the Rocky Mountains, Great Plains, Great Lakes, Mississippi River, and vast Arctic tundra—alongside megacities like New York, Los Angeles, Mexico City, and Toronto. Rich in oil, minerals, freshwater, and farmland, North America has shaped global culture through its technology, entertainment, and political influence, while hosting extraordinary biodiversity from polar bears to jaguars.
